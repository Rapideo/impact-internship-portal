Welcome

Getting into the test environment

The IMPACT portal tracks interns through your program — from intake and entry assessment, through competency and self-assessments, to 90- and 180-day employment outcomes. This guide walks the admin (program-staff) experience so you can test every area.

The three kinds of user

Admin

Program staff

Full access — manages employers, cohorts, interns, assessments, reports, and accounts. This is the role you'll test with.

Employer

Partner orgs

Signs in to see only their own cohorts and interns, and to complete competency & exit surveys for them.

Intern

Anonymous

No login. Confirms identity (initial + last name + cohort) and completes three one-time self-assessments.

03
Settings

Employers & cohorts

Your program's structure is built in Settings → Employers. An employer has roles (the kinds of placement they offer) and cohorts (a group of interns running over a date range). Cohorts are where interns live.

Employer detail — cohorts, roles & account access

The hierarchy:

Employer → Role → Cohort → Intern

  • Open any employer row to see its cohorts and roles.
  • + New Cohort / + New Role add to that employer.
  • Each cohort selects which phases apply (see §7).
  • Account access invites that employer to sign in and self-serve their assessments.
Settings → Employers — the full partner list (rows are clickable)
Try this Click + New Employer, add a fictional org, then open it and add a role and a cohort. You've just built a place to put interns.
04
Interns

Interns & their records

The Interns tab is the roster. Each intern record is a single page that holds intake, the entry assessment, self-assessment status, evaluations, and employment outcomes.

A full intern record

On the record you'll find:

  • Identity & placement — locked after creation.
  • Entry Assessment — barriers at intake + notes.
  • Self-Assessments — which of the three the intern has submitted.
  • Evaluations — competency & exit-survey status.
  • Employment Details — 90-day & 180-day outcome checkboxes + notes.
Minimum-PII by design Only first initial + last name + cohort are stored. The create form accepts a first name for convenience but saves just the initial.
Try this Open an intern, tick Employed at 90 days, add a note, and Save — then watch the 90-day number move on Home and Reports.
05
Assessments

Competency & exit-employer surveys

From the Assessments tab, admins complete two kinds of evaluation on behalf of employers: the Competency Rubric and the Exit Employer Survey. You pick an intern, then fill the form.

The competency rubric — 7 shared professional domains plus role-specific skills
How it works Each domain is rated Emerging / Developing / Ready with room for notes. Competency is phase-specific — the phase dropdown is filtered to the intern's cohort.
Three-tier rubric Questions stitch together at assessment time: program-wide Core 7 + per-cohort role skills + any per-intern items (authored in Settings → Questions).
Try this Start a competency assessment from the Assessments tab (pick any intern), rate a few domains, and submit. Then reopen the intern to see the evaluation recorded.
06
Assessments

Intern self-assessments

Interns complete their own reflections through a public, login-free flow. They confirm their identity once, then complete up to three one-time forms: Personal Goals, Midpoint Reflection, and Participant Feedback.

The intern's identity chooser (public)
  • Identity = first initial + last name + employer + cohort.
  • Each form is one submission per intern and immutable once sent.
  • Admins review every submission under Assessments → Self-assessment results.
Heads up Because self-assessments are permanent, test them in staging only — never on the live site.
Admin view — submitted self-assessments
Try this From the test URL, open /intern/assessments, pick an employer and cohort, enter an intern's initial + last name, and walk through Personal Goals.
07
Reports

The reports dashboard

Reports turns the data into program metrics. Use the Viewing filters at the top to scope everything from program-wide down to a single employer or cohort.

Program reports — KPIs, employment gauges, completion meters, barriers & submissions trend
  • KPI tiles — employers, active interns, 90-day employment, % assessed.
  • Employment Outcomes — 90- and 180-day gauges.
  • Assessment Completion — how far each assessment type has progressed.
  • Entry Barriers & Submissions Over Time round out the picture.
Try this Set Viewing to a single employer and watch every chart re-scope; then click Program-wide to reset.
08
Settings

Settings & user management

Settings is where you configure the program and manage who can sign in. The left rail lists every area; Users is the newest.

Settings → Users — admin & employer accounts

Users lets you:

  • Create an admin or employer account (employer accounts attach to an employer).
  • Set a password now, or send an invite email.
  • Change a role and deactivate / reactivate an account (reversible).

Known limitations on staging
  • Invite & password-reset emails don't deliver yet — use “set a password now.”
  • Role changes apply on the user's next login, not instantly.
  • Test data may be refreshed/reset between rounds — don't rely on anything you enter persisting.
When something's wrong Note what you did, what you expected, and what happened — a screenshot and the page URL help a lot. Send it to Matt by your agreed channel.
